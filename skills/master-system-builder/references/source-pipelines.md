# Source pipeline selection

Inventory first. Process a small representative sample before scaling. Preserve stable provider IDs, dates, URLs, speaker roles, and content hashes.

## Email

- Use a user-authorized export, connector, or forwarded mailbox.
- Filter by sender, domain, subject, date, and labels.
- Preserve headers needed for provenance; remove tracking parameters, repetitive footers, and unrelated personal data.
- Deduplicate with stable message IDs and content hashes.
- Separate instructional content from pure promotion.
- Never publish private email or subscriber data with the Skill.

## YouTube and public video

- Inventory official channels, playlists, interviews, and guest appearances.
- Prefer human captions, then official automatic captions, then local transcription when lawful.
- Preserve video ID, channel, publication date, URL, and timestamps.
- Identify the actual speaker. An official channel does not make every presenter the expert.
- Treat clips, mirrors, and repeated playlist embeds as references to one media object.

## Podcasts

- Prefer official RSS feeds and publisher pages.
- Preserve episode GUID, enclosure URL, show, date, speakers, and timestamps.
- Deduplicate cross-platform copies.
- Use publisher transcripts when available; otherwise transcribe lawfully obtained audio locally.

## Websites and social posts

- Prefer the author's official site or first-party account.
- Record canonical URL, author, publication date, and collection date.
- Preserve version evidence when pages can change.
- Do not treat search snippets or reposts as primary sources.

## Books

- Confirm the user's access rights.
- Preserve edition, chapter, and page anchors.
- Keep limited quotations and notes separate from full copyrighted text.
- Never reconstruct missing author rules from reviews or third-party implementations.

## Courses and member content

- Require the human to complete login, purchase, forms, and consent.
- Do not bypass DRM or platform restrictions.
- Inventory courses, sections, lessons, attachments, and stable IDs before capture.
- A lesson may contain multiple attachments; use attachment IDs rather than titles as keys.
- Save permitted downloads only. When media cannot be retained, keep a private index and timestamped notes that point back to the platform.
- Prefer audio-only processing when visuals are unnecessary and the platform permits it.
- Keep cookies, tokens, user IDs, and signed URLs outside durable state.

## Webinars and live sessions

- Record event, date, speakers, source, and access basis.
- Separate teaching, Q&A, guest speech, and sales segments.
- Use expert Q&A as reasoning evidence and sales demonstrations as performance examples.

## Unknown source types

1. Describe the source and ownership.
2. Establish access and redistribution boundaries.
3. Build an inventory.
4. Choose the least invasive method with sufficient fidelity.
5. Test one item.
6. Validate the output.
7. Record a reusable local procedure before batch work.
