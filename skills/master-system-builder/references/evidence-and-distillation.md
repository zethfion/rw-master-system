# Evidence and distillation

## Map stage

Create one distillate per source or coherent small batch. Record:

- source path and stable evidence anchors;
- explicit claims;
- named methods, conditions, and steps;
- examples and counterexamples;
- repeated vocabulary or reasoning habits;
- conflicts and changes over time;
- candidate framework IDs;
- open questions.

Automatic transcripts are navigation aids until important names, numbers, thresholds, and corrections are checked against audio, video, slides, or another primary source.

## Reduce stage

Build the expert profile from distillates, not model memory. Weight evidence by purpose:

- Use doctrinal sources for formal methods.
- Use conversational sources for natural reasoning and values.
- Use performative sources for craft and channel-specific style.

Do not let polished marketing copy define the expert's entire personality.

## Framework card

```markdown
---
master: expert-id
framework: framework-id
type: decision-or-output-type
maturity: raw-notes
sources:
  - corpus/category/source.md#anchor
---

# Framework name

One sentence: what problem this method solves.

## Use when

## Do not use when

## Preconditions

## Steps

For every step, state the action, reason, completion test, and evidence anchor.

## Parameters and variants

## Counterexamples and failure modes

## Evidence status

Separate explicit statements, affiliated claims, secondary implementations, inferences, and unknowns.

## Validation history
```

Use maturity values:

- `raw-notes`: promising but insufficiently checked.
- `distilled`: supported and stable enough for consultation.
- `battle-tested`: used in a documented real task; this describes local validation, not a guarantee by the expert.

## Soul profile

Include:

1. Identity and time coverage.
2. Core beliefs with evidence.
3. Reasoning and diagnostic habits.
4. Repeated objections and tradeoffs.
5. Natural voice and channel-specific differences.
6. Known incentives, biases, and market context.
7. Topics not covered by the corpus.
8. A disclosure that unsupported answers are inference or unknown.

Avoid theatrical impersonation, isolated catchphrases, and certainty beyond the evidence.

## Conflicts

Keep competing versions with dates and sources. Do not collapse disagreement unless a later primary source explicitly supersedes an earlier one. If the evidence cannot resolve the conflict, expose it to the user.
